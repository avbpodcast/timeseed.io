Why a second layer

Trust high-level encryption and key generation, not the app.

Every messenger asks you to trust its servers, its updates, its leadership, their “end-to-end (e2e) encryption” theater. “The Sovereign” TimeSeed removes that single point of failure: your message is already encrypted before it ever touches their apps, network or servers.

🔑

TimeSeed

A strong 50-character key, generated fresh on your own device. It encrypts your messages — and the encrypted text can travel over any messenger you already use. Same key format as timeseed.io/v3/.

🌶

Pepper passwords

Ten short secret words you and one contact share. They are an optional extra lock you can add on top of TimeSeed encryption — use any of them, as often as you like. There is no limit.

🃏

The Contact Card

One card for each pair of people. It holds both of your TimeSeed keys, the pepper words you share, and how to reach each other. Made together, face to face — you each keep an identical copy.

After setup

Using The Sovereign in real life.

You and your contact have made one shared card and you each keep a copy. From here on, sending a private message takes four steps.

1

Write your message

Type it out as you normally would. Nothing secret has happened yet — this is just your plain message.

2

Encrypt it with TimeSeed

Open the TimeSeed tool — at timeseed.io, or the generator built into the wizard above. It encrypts your message with your key. If you want a second lock, add one of your shared pepper passwords too — that part is optional.

3

Send it over any messenger

TimeSeed turns your message into a block of unreadable text. Send that block however you two already talk — WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal, WhiteNoise, WeChat, email, anything at all. Whoever intercepts it sees only noise.

4

Your contact decrypts it

They paste the block into their own TimeSeed tool and unlock it with the matching key — plus the same pepper word, if you added one. Your real message appears.

Pepper passwords are optional. They add a second lock on top of TimeSeed encryption — and there is no limit: reuse them, mix them, use them for as many messages as you like. Leave them out and TimeSeed alone still keeps your message private.
DENMS is a separate tool. TimeSeed already works on its own, over any messenger. But if you would like one dedicated app that bundles encryption and sending together, DENMS has its own page — allesvoorbitcoin.be/21tools/DENMS/.

A real example: Alice and Bob

One shared card, used however and whenever they like.

Over coffee

Alice and Bob fill in the wizard together: both codenames, a TimeSeed key for each of them, and 10 shared pepper words. They print two copies of the finished card — one each — and leave with identical cards.

Any day

Alice writes “Locked in for Friday.” She encrypts it with her TimeSeed, adds pepper word 3 as an extra lock, and sends the scrambled text to Bob over WhatsApp. Bob pastes it into his TimeSeed tool and decrypts it with his copy of the card.

And again

Next time Bob replies the same way — over Telegram instead, this time. He can reuse a pepper word or pick another; nothing runs out and there is no limit on how many messages they send.

Plain words

Every term, explained simply.

No background needed. Here is every word this page uses, in one plain sentence each.

The Sovereign
The name for this whole approach: taking your message security into your own hands, instead of trusting an app to do it for you.
Second layer
Your message is locked by you before it ever enters a messaging app. The app may add its own lock too — but only you and your contact hold the key to the inner one.
TimeSeed
A 50-character key made on your own device. It encrypts your messages; the encrypted text can then travel over any messenger.
Pepper password
An optional extra secret word, shared with your contact. Adding one puts a second lock on a message. There is no limit on how many messages you can use peppers for.
Contact Card
One card for a pair of people. It holds both people's TimeSeed keys, your shared pepper words, and how to reach each other. You make it together, in person, and each keep an identical copy.
Different messaging methods
The Sovereign's encrypted text can be sent over any app or network you already use — WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal, WhiteNoise, WeChat, email and so on.
DENMS
A separate, dedicated tool that bundles encryption and messaging together. It has its own page; The Sovereign works fine without it.
